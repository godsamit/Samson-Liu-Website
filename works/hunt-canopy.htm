Hunt Canopy

QUICK DESIGN WORKSHOP

Project Date: 2015 Spring | Individual

DIGITAL MEDIA

This two-week design exercise was integrated into my first-year digital media course, when I was just starting to learn to render and use Photoshop. The goal of the project is to integrate digital media into the iterative design process, and to use digitally generated images to explain and communicate my ideas.

The project is to design a new canopy for CMU's Hunt Library. I started the design wanting to keep the original fin pattern on the facade, while also introduce more dynamic and fluid geometries to the big rigid box. Looking from facing the fins, the new canopy almost disappears and blends in with the existing building, but from the side it forms a huge contrast.

Renders were generated quickly to test and share different ideas. The final renders are among the first images I generated in Photoshop. A model was also built to test how "invisible" the structure truly was from certain angles.
