Project Date: 2017 Fall TA | Individual

DESIGNING AN ASSIGNMENT

This is one of the projects I took on as a teaching assistant to the first-year digital media class, where I did the Hunt Canopy Project

For this project, the professor is interested in using a hybrid workflow, mixing analog and digital media, to teach diagramming and basic Photoshop techniques through a small-scale design project. I was responsible for site selection and documentation. After some discussion about the programs and deliverables, I was then solely responsible to create 2 sets of in-class tutorial slides and sample submission.

The assignment task is to design a bookstore/cafe/office building in downtown Pittsburgh. Students were expected to analyze the site and come up with 2 fundamentally different schemes. Students should use a mixture of handdrawn and digital media to quickly generate diagrams and renders. For the 3d view specifically, students were expected to match the rhino viewport to given photos, then export and trace over, and render the view.

After discussing the grading rubrics, I did the assignment myself and documented each step. I made the in-class slides, which consists of assignment clearifications, concept explanations and step-by-step tutorials.
