Project Date: 2017 Fall TA | Individual

DESIGNING AN ASSIGNMENT

This is one of the projects I took on as a teaching assistant to the first-year Digital Media class, where I did the Hunt Canopy Project

For this project, Professor Kim was interested in using a hybrid workflow, mixing analog and digital media, to teach diagramming and basic Photoshop techniques. I was responsible for site selection and documentation, which includes taking site photos and providing a site model and basic template drawings. After some discussion about the programs and deliverables, I was then responsible to create 2 sets of in-class, step-by-step tutorial slides, and a sample submission.

The assignment task is to generate preliminary designs and renders for a bookstore/cafe/office building in downtown Pittsburgh. The assignment is divided up into 2 sections:

Diagrams and elevations. Students are introduced to the basic concepts of diagramming. They are then asked to analyze the site conditions, and to digram factors that may affect their design decisions. Finally, the students are asked to generate two design schemes and evaluate them through simple elevations. 2D and 3D Rendering. Students are asked to render their elevations. They are also asked to generate perspective renderings from the street level. Since 2-point perspective has not been taught, we asked the students to choose from the site photos, bring it into Rhino, match the view, then print out the viewport for tracing over.
Throughout the entire process, we are encouraging students to get used to the "hybrid workflow", to get the best of both worlds. For example, with the help of Rhino viewport, the first-years were able to generate barebone but complex perspectives quickly. Then the students could use analog techniques to efficiently add in more details. This enables students to generate quicker and better representations to evaluate their own designs.
