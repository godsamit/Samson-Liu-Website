Aqua Pavilion

INTERACTIVE RAINWATER COLLECTION

Project Date: 2015 Fall Studio | Collaborative


DESIGN INTENT AND PROCESS

This project was a one-week design competition. The prompt was to design a water-collection shed for the MLK community garden in Pittsburgh, operated by a local Non-profit, Grow Pittsburgh. To familiarize ourselves with the site, we went and helped out for a day, learning how the community garden helped unify the local community.

Our top goal was to make rainwater collection more interactive, as something the community can touch and play with, rather than something isolate structure that only exists on the roof. From the start, we had an idea for a "rain-waterfall" that makes the collection process visible to the community. Therefore, we decided to utilize the golden curve into our design, since we liked the "shrinking" quality of it. Also, for a short-term project, the golden curve served as a geometry basis to structure and filter our teammates' different ideas.

The final design features the aforementioned "waterfall". The descending heights also made some areas only accessible to children. We also integrated a "hydraulophone" in our design, to make the collection more interactive and engaging. Through our effort to make the collection more visible to the community, we hope to raise awareness of recycling water in the community.

This project came second in the competition.
