Aqua Pavilion

INTERACTIVE RAINWATER COLLECTION

Project date: 2015 Fall
Project type: Competition (one-week) | Collaborative | Second place
Project prompt: Design a water-collecting pavilion for the local Martin Luther-King Garden.
My responsibilities: Ideation | Digital modeling | Digital rendering
What I learned: Rapid prototyping | Design coordination

DESIGN INTENT AND PROCESS

This project was a one-week design competition conducted within groups of 4. The goal was to design a water-collection shed for the MLK community garden in Pittsburgh, operated by a local Non-profit, Grow Pittsburgh. To familiarize ourselves with the site, we went and helped out for a day, learning how the community garden helped unify the local community.

Our primary goal was to make rainwater collection more interactive, as something the community can touch and play with, instead of an isolate structure on the roof. From the start, we had an idea for a "rain-waterfall" that makes the collection process visible to the community. What's more, we also envisioned to integrate a "hydraulophone" in our design, to make the collection more interactive and engaging.

The final design utilized the golden curve to serve both as a geometry basis and a filter for our teammates' different ideas. The golden curve was used on two levels:
As an organizing curve for the frames. We arranged frames along the curve. As geometric references to the frames themselves.
The frames are continually scaled down as the curve spirals into itself, thus creating the difference in height, as the "waterfall". The descending heights also made some areas only accessible to children.

This project came second in the competition.
